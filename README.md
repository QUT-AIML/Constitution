<a name="name"></a>
## 1. Name

**1.1** The name of the Club shall be **QUT AI & ML Society (QUT AIML)** (the Club).

---

<a name="objectives"></a>
## 2. Objectives

**2.1** The objectives of the Club shall be to:

a. Gain insights and guidance from alumni, professionals, and leaders in the AI and ML industry.  
b. Collaborate, experiment, and build impactful projects focused on artificial intelligence and machine learning.  
c. Connect and network with students, researchers, and professionals in the AI, ML, and broader tech community.

---

<a name="affiliation"></a>
## 3. Affiliation

**3.1** The Club shall be affiliated with the **QUT Student Guild (the Guild)**.

**3.2** The Club must, as a condition of its affiliation, enter into and comply with an **Affiliation Agreement** set by the Guild each year.

**3.3** The Guild may suspend or terminate its affiliation with the Club for a breach of this Constitution or the Affiliation Agreement.

---

<a name="membership"></a>
## 4. Membership

### Categories of Membership

**4.1** The Club shall offer annual membership as outlined in its Constitution. These categories are determined by enrolment status at the Queensland University of Technology (QUT) at the time of application:

i. Student Membership is open to individuals who are enrolled at QUT.

---

### Conditions of Membership

**4.2** Membership is subject to the provisions of this Constitution and the Affiliation Agreement.

**4.3** The Executive may decline a membership application or revoke membership where the individual:

i. has engaged in conduct, as a member of the Club, that has breached this Constitution or the Affiliation Agreement; or  
ii. has engaged in conduct, as a member of another Guild affiliated body, that resulted in their suspension or expulsion; or  
iii. is otherwise deemed to pose a risk to the safety, reputation, or lawful operation of the Club.

**4.4** The Executive must provide the individual with written notice of a decision to decline membership application or revoke their membership, including the reason for the decision, within seven (7) days of the determination.

**4.5** An individual who has been notified of a decision to decline their membership application or revoke their membership has the right to appeal the decision in writing to a general meeting within seven (7) days of being notified.

---

### Membership Fees

**4.6** The Executive may set an annual membership fee for each membership category or determine that membership is free.

**4.7** Where an annual membership fee is set, it is payable upon application or renewal and is non-refundable, except as required by law.

**4.8** Membership rights commence upon payment of the applicable annual membership fee in full.

---

### Membership Register

**4.9** The Club must maintain a membership register that includes the names, membership category, student number (where applicable), and contact details of all members.

---

### Cessation of Membership

**4.10** Membership rights cease when a member:

i. resigns in writing to the Secretary;  
ii. fails to renew their membership by paying the applicable fees by the due date;  
iii. ceases to meet eligibility requirements for their membership category; or  
iv. is expelled under the disciplinary conditions of this Constitution or the Affiliation Agreement.

---

<a name="executive"></a>
## 5. Executive

### Composition

**5.1** The Executive shall consist of:

i. President  
ii. Secretary  
iii. Treasurer  
iv. All other positions established on the QUT AIML GitHub Role Descriptions Page

---

### Eligibility

**5.2** Only Student Members are eligible to hold Executive positions.

---

### Powers of the Executive

**5.3** The Executive of the Club shall:

i. Act in accordance with the aims and objectives of the Club and at all times.  
ii. Be subject to the decisions of any GM of the Club.  
iii. Remain subject at all times to the decisions of any General Meeting (GM) of the Club.  
iv. Make regulations necessary to give effect to this Constitution.  
v. Make or amend policy decisions, which shall be binding unless altered or reversed by a GM of the Club.  
vi. Conduct activities in the name of the Club and arrange all activities of the Club.  
vii. Fill casual vacancies arising during its term of office.  
viii. Keep accurate records of all proceedings of the Club.  
ix. Manage and keep records of all finances of the Club.  
x. Ensure compliance with the terms of the Affiliation Agreement.

---

### Duties of Executives

#### President

**5.4** The President must:

i. provide leadership and strategic direction for the Club;  
ii. convene and chair all General Meetings and Executive Meetings;  
iii. represent the Club in all official matters in line with its aims and objectives;  
iv. exercise urgent powers between meetings where necessary, subject to ratification at the next meeting of the Executive;  
v. hold the casting vote at meetings where votes are tied; and  
vi. any other duties as assigned by the Executive.

---

#### Secretary

**5.5** The Secretary must:

i. prepare and distribute notices and agendas of General Meetings and Executive Meetings;  
ii. record and maintain accurate minutes of all meetings;  
iii. maintain the Club’s official records, including membership register and correspondence;  
iv. ensure compliance with all affiliation requirements, reporting requirements and relevant legislation; and  
v. carry out any other duties as assigned by the Executive.

---

#### Treasurer

**5.6** The Treasurer must:

i. manage the financial affairs of the Club responsibly and in accordance with the approved budget;  
ii. maintain accurate and up-to-date financial records;  
iii. prepare and present financial reports to the Executive and General Meetings;  
iv. oversee the preparation and submission of budgets, grant applications, and acquittals; and  
v. carry out any other duties as assigned by the Executive.

---

#### Other Executive Roles

**5.7** The other roles established on the QUT AIML GitHub role description page must:

i. Perform the roles and responsibilities as outlined in the QUT AIML GitHub Role Descriptions page;  
ii. Act in accordance with the aims and objectives of the Club;  
iii. Operate under the direction of, and remain accountable to, the Executive;  
iv. Carry out any other duties as assigned by the Executive.

---

<a name="elections"></a>
## 6. Elections

### Nominations

**6.1** Nominations for Executive positions must be called at least fourteen (14) days before the Annual General Meeting.

**6.2** Nominations for Executive positions must be submitted in writing to the Secretary no later than seven (7) days before the Annual General Meeting.

**6.3** If no eligible nominations for an Executive position are received, nominations for that position may be called for and accepted from the floor at the Annual General Meeting, subject to the nominee meeting the eligibility requirements under this Constitution.

**6.4** If multiple eligible nominations for an Executive position are received, the Executive position is deemed contested and must proceed to an election at the Annual General Meeting.

---

### Election Management

**6.5** The President shall act as the Electoral Officer unless they are a nominee for any position, in which case another Executive Member will be appointed as Electoral Officer by the Annual General Meeting. If all Executive Members are nominees, a Student Member shall be appointed as Electoral Officer by the Annual General Meeting.

**6.6** The Electoral Officer is responsible for conducting the election and declaring its results.

**6.7** Voting must be conducted by secret ballot or a show of hands of Student Members present at the Annual General Meeting.

**6.8** In the event of a tie, the election shall be decided by a re-vote between the tied candidates. If the re-vote is also tied, the Electoral Officer shall determine the outcome by lot (random draw).

---

### Vacancies

**6.9** If any Executive position remains vacant after the Annual General Meeting or becomes vacant between Annual General Meetings, the Executive may appoint a Student Member to fill the role for the remainder of the term.

---

### Removal

**6.10** An Executive may be removed from office before the end of their term by a two-thirds (⅔) majority vote of a General Meeting.

---

### Term of Office

**6.11** The term of office for Executives is from **1 December to 30 November** of the following year.

---

<a name="financial-management"></a>
## 7. Financial Management

### Financial Year

**7.1** The financial year of the Club shall run from **1 July to 30 June** the following year.

---

### Use of Funds

**7.2** All Club funds must be used solely for purposes consistent with the objectives of the Club.

**7.3** No income or property of the Club may be paid or transferred directly or indirectly to any member, except where payment is made for goods or services provided to the Club on terms approved by the Executive.

---

### Financial Records

**7.4** The Club must maintain accurate and up-to-date records of all income, expenditure, assets, and liabilities.

---

### Budgeting

**7.5** The Executive must prepare and approve an Annual Budget prior to the start of each financial year.

---

### Reporting

**7.6** The Executive must prepare and present a financial report to the Annual General Meeting.

---

### Winding Up

**7.7** In the event that the Club is formally wound up, and after the payment of all debts and liabilities, any surplus assets remaining shall not be distributed to members. Surplus assets must be transferred to the Guild, or to another not-for-profit organisation with similar objectives, as determined by the Executive and approved by the Guild.

**7.8** In the event that the Club had been inactive for a continuous period of six (6) months, the Guild may deem the Club to be formally wound up. In the absence of a functioning Executive, after payment of debts and liabilities, any surplus assets shall not be distributed to members. Surplus assets must be transferred to the Guild, or to another not-for-profit organisation with similar objects, as determined by the Guild.

---

<a name="general-meetings"></a>
## 8. General Meetings

### Annual General Meetings (AGM)

**8.1** The Club must hold an AGM once each year between **1 August and 31 October**.

**8.2** At least fourteen (14) days’ written notice of the AGM must be provided to all members, specifying the date, time, location, and business to be considered.

**8.3** The business of the AGM shall include:

i. confirmation of the minutes of the previous AGM;  
ii. presentation of the President’s Report;  
iii. presentation of the Treasurer’s Report;  
iv. election of the Executive for the following year; and  
v. any constitutional amendments or other business of which notice has been given.

---

### Special General Meetings (SGM)

**8.4** An SGM may be called by the Executive or by written request of at least twenty (20) Student Members or one-quarter (¼) of the Student Membership, whichever is less.

**8.5** At least seven (7) days’ written notice of an SGM must be provided to all members, specifying the date, time, location, and business to be considered.

**8.6** Only business specified in the notice may be transacted at an SGM.

---

### Quorum

**8.7** The quorum for all General Meetings is twenty (20) Student Members or one-quarter (¼) of the Student Membership, whichever is less.

**8.8** If a quorum is not present within thirty (30) minutes of the scheduled start time, the meeting shall be adjourned to a date not more than fourteen (14) days later, and notice shall be given to all members.

---

### Voting Rights

**8.9** Only Student Members have the right to move motions and vote at General Meetings.

**8.10** Each Student Member has one (1) vote, which must be exercised in person. Proxy voting is not permitted.

**8.11** Unless otherwise specified in this Constitution, motions are passed by a simple majority (more than 50%) of Student Members present and voting.

---

<a name="constitutional-amendments"></a>
## 9. Constitutional Amendments

### Approval

**9.1** The Club must first submit any proposed amendments to this Constitution in writing to the Guild for review and approval.

**9.2** The Guild will assess whether the proposed amendments are consistent with requirements as determined by the Guild.

**9.3** If the Guild approves the proposed amendments, the Secretary must provide notice of the proposed amendments, including the Guild’s approval, to all members at least seven (7) days before the General Meeting at which it is to be considered.

**9.4** Amendments to this Constitution require a majority vote of Student Members present and voting at a General Meeting.

---

### Adoption

**9.5** Amendments take effect at the conclusion of the General Meeting.

---
